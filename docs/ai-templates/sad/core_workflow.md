This is the complete, end-to-end product workflow for our **AI-Powered Expense Reimbursement System** . This document explains the roles of the Employee and HR, the logic of the AI Agent, and how the system prevents fraud while saving time. 

## **1. Employee Workflow: The "Smart Entry" Phase** 

The goal for the employee is to move from **Creation** to **Correction** , reducing the time spent on manual data entry by 90%. 

- **Step 1: Initiation:** The employee selects a claim category (e.g., Business Travel) and uploads up to **10 supporting documents** (Receipts, Invoices, or PDFs). 

- **Step 2: AI OCR Processing:** The AI immediately processes each document, extracting key data: Merchant Name, Date, Currency, Line-Item amounts, etc (e.g., distinguishing between Meals, Transport, and Accommodation). 

- **Step 3: Side-by-Side Verification:** 

   - **Left Column:** An editable form pre-filled by the AI. 

   - **Right Column:** A live document preview. 

   - **The "Fraud Trap":** If an employee manually changes an amount (e.g., the AI found RM 20, but the employee types RM 200), the system silently flags this **Discrepancy** in the audit trail. → HUMAN EDITED VS ORIGINAL 

- **Step 4: Submission:** Once the employee confirms the data, the AI runs a **Policy Check & Others Check** (e.g., "Is there alcohol on this receipt?", "Does the hotel exceed the RM 500 limit?"). 

## **2. AI Audit Layer: The "Digital Gatekeeper"** 

Before HR even sees the claim, the AI categorizes it based on risk and compliance: 

- **Bucket A: Passed AI Review:** High-confidence claims with no policy violations and zero discrepancies between AI extraction and employee input. 

- **Bucket B: Requires Attention:** Claims with policy flags (e.g., alcohol detected), high discrepancy (fraud risk), or low OCR confidence (blurry images). 

## **3. HR Workflow: The "Audit & Decision" Phase & Add Policy** 

HR no longer "types" or "checks" every single claim. They perform **Efficiency by Exception** , focusing only on the red flags. 

## **Detailed View (For both Buckets)** 

The UI is split to maximize decision-making speed: 

## **A. Left Column: The Evidence & Audit Trail** 

1. **Employee Identity & Claim Contexts:** Basic info (Employee Name, Department, Date). 

2. **AI Form:** The final data submitted by the employee. 

3. **Financial Breakdown :** Every receipt that has been uploaded by the employee including description and AI modifying action. 

4. **Policy Flags:** Clear alerts generated by the AI (e.g., ⚠ _"Alcohol detected in Receipt #3"_ ). 

5. **Audit Trail:** A log showing exactly what the AI extracted vs. what the employee edited. 

## **B. Right Column: The Decision Engine** 

1. **AI Fraud Analysis:** A confidence score and reasoning (e.g., _"85% Fraud Risk: Amount edited by user is 10x higher than OCR extraction"_ ). 

**2. Financial Summary** 

**3. Per Receipt Decision** : Edit or modify amount for each receipt 

4. **Multi-Receipt Decision Logic:** HR can process complex claims with 10 receipts using three actions: 

   - **Force Full Approval:** Approve the total requested amount. 

   - **Approve Adjusted Amount:** Approve adjusted amount from Per Receipt Decision 

   - **Confirm Rejection:** Reject the entire claim. 

5. **HR Note:** Comments explaining the final decision to the employee. 

## **HR Policy Upload** 

HR can add the main policy and appendix. Then AI will analyze it. AI will give a summary, what kind of policy it is and give a list of mandatory constraints or SOP checklists from that policy. 

HR Also can upload some of the policy as the AI baseline to think and decide reimbursement analysis. The HR also can do versioning of the policy. Moreover it can also edit, add, and delete the mandatory constraint (SOP Checklist). 

## **4. Final Output: The Single Source of Truth** 

Once HR submits their decision: 

1. **Status Sync:** The claim moves to **Approved** , **Partially Approved** , or **Rejected** . 

2. **PDF Generation:** The system generates the **Official Claim Form (PDF)** . This document contains the final approved amounts and the HR/AI audit notes. 

3. **Data Archiving:** The raw receipts are archived for internal reference, but the **Official PDF** becomes the legal document for finance and tax audits.
